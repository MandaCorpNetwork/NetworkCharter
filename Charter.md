# 1. GENERAL PURPOSE / MISSION STATEMENT

**The Unnamed Alliance (the "Alliance")** exists to create a **secure, collaborative, and mutually beneficial network of player-run organisations** across *Star Citizen*. Our core objectives are to:

1. **Promote collective security** - defend member territories, share intelligence, and coordinate responses to external threats.__
2. **Foster economic prosperity** - enable free, fair, mutually benificial, and trasparent trade, resource sharing, and joint infrastructure projects.__
3. **Encourage community growth** - provide a welcoming environment for new players, mentor newcomers, and assist players in finding a group that inspires them to have higher playtime and participation rates.__
4. **Provide Collective Intercommunication** - a system of communication between organisations, individuals and crews of people for consistent and connected gameplay.__
5. **Centralize Loyalty** - Ensuring all organisations, groups and individuals have a strong loyalty to the larger collective.

*All actions taken by the Alliance and its members must further these aims.*__

## 2. GENERAL COMMITMENTS

### 2.1. Aid
* **Defensive Aid** - Members agree to provide timely assistance (troops, reinforcements, or strategic support [as available]) when any member is under attack, provided the request follows the **Aid Request Procedure** (Section 4.1).__
* **Humanitarian Aid** - In the event of a catastrophic loss of resources (e.g., a raide that destroys a major base), a member may request emergency supplies. The Alliance will allocate a **relief pool** on a rotating-priority basis, suplemented by the Central Hub Bank.__
* **Administrative Aid** - For groups or organisations that request, the Hub will provide Administrative tools, training & infrastructure to assist with Individual Community Management and Operations.__
* **Relations Aid** - Assist in conflict resolution, outreach, and public relations on the behalf of other members.__

### 2.2. Economic Partner
* **Open-Market Principle** - All members may trade any in-game commodity or resource unless explicityle prohibited by the Code of Conduct (Section 3).__
* **Resource-Sharing Pools** - Each member may contribute a voluntary percentage of their surplus to the **Common Supply Depot** that can be drawn upon by any member with a valid request.__
* **Joint Projects** - The Alliance may establish **Base-Sharing Agreements**, **Mining/Harvesting Coalitions**, or **Infrastructure Networks** (e.g., shared transport routes). Such projects must be approved via the **Project Approval Process** (Section 4.5).__
* **Central Hub Bank** - The Alliance Hub will maintain a Central Bank system that maintains an Alliance Treasury that maintains the Common Supply Depot, Central Currency Bank, & Alliance Stocks.

### 2.3. Community Growth Support
* **New-Commer Support** - New Members to any Organisations should be treated as individuals, with their highest level of enjoyment of participation taken into mind when recruiting or interacting with.__
* **Member Playstyle Preference** - Player Playstyle Prefernce should be taken into account when attempting to Onboard New Members to individuals Member Orgs. If there is a better Org, Crew, or Group that may suit their playstyle more, they should be properly directed to those communities.__
* **Player Relocation** - If a player has requested to switch their Primary Org Membership to another Member Org, the player should be allowed to do so if the Org they are requested to move to has given proper internal sign-offs.__
* **Event Participation** - Member Orgs are to participate in Alliance Level Events & Member Org Hosted Events when able.__
* **Community Access** - All Member Orgs should Maintain a Directory of Member Org Communites, Hub Website & Tools, Shared Training Documentation, and Intercommunication Channels that all certified members have access to.__

## 3. CODE OF CONDUCT

### 3.1. Expectations of Member Organisations (Org-Level)
- Uphold Alliance objectives by promoting security, prosperity and growth within their ranks.
- Assign roles responsibly, ensuring that their Leaders are of quality stock.
- Avoid trying to drown out the collective leadership of the Network.
- Report violations promptly to Network Directors.
- Understand structure and usage of Network Tools and to promote and teach their usage to their members.
- Properly maintain connection access to Network Member Organizations.
- Avoid confilcts of intests.
- Maintain transparency of operations to Network Admiral Council, Network Board of Directors, & Network Developers where applicable.
- Respect Network Hub's claim on Non-Primary-Org players, and Small Org (sub 10 player) aquisitions as Network Crews unless they are activly seeking to join your Org as a Crew or individuals.

### 3.2. Expectations of Crew Members (Crew-Level)
- Participate in Network aid requests when feasible, prioritizing Alliance loyalty over individual gains.
- Respect inter-crew boundries. Avoid poaching members from other active crews, leading to structural decay of Operational Readiness of other crews.
- Treat all players respectfully, regardless of org or crew affiliation.

### 3.3. Expectations of Individual Members (Player-Level)
- Demonstrate loyalty to the Network by engaging in events and supporting mutual aid.
- Treat all players respectfully, regardless of org or crew affiliations.
    > You don't all have to get along or like each other, but you do have to keep your mouth shut and not start shit.
- If you see something, say something
- Properly utilize Network Tools
- Prioritize fun and immersion

### 3.4. Interaction Between Different Alliance Orgs
- Communicate properly utilizing Hub intercommunication channels and tools
- Resolve disputes informally first, escalating only if necessary
- Share files and resources transparently
- Collaborate on joint projects
- Avoid aggressive recruitment from other orgs.
    > You should be pushing Members that you feel belong in another group to Relocate rather than try to convince members from other communities to join you instead. This ensures our Member Orgs foster a sense of Mutual Loyalty and support

### 3.5. Interaction Between Different Crews & Org to Crew
- Respect Needs-Based assignment during Network or Non-Parent Organization participation.
- Orgs must oversee crew activities and engagement
- If Member Orgs opt into the Crew System, they should layout clearly to their members who qualifies for crews
- Member Orgs can utilize the Crew System however they wish
- Prevent internal rivalries from affecting Alliance-wide operations
- Facilitate cross-crew teams
- Encourage crews to reach out and participate outside their Parent-Org to promote activity

### 3.6 Interaction Between Individuals with other Member Orgs & Crews
- Engage positively
- Avoid harassment
- Orgs should not force Guest Crews to play outside their primary focus

## 4. PROCESSES & PROCEDURES

### 4.1. New User Onboarding

#### 4.1.1. Primary Org Onboarding
A New Member can join an Org via Discord or through the Network Hub Website. Regardless on entry method, the process will be the same.
1. After joining, an Onboarding Ticket will be created.
2. The new user will have to fill out information, including their Star Citizen Handle, what Org they are attempting to join (which will default to whatever discord they joined), and what membership they are requesting (i.e. Guest, Partner, Member)
3. The user will be prompted to Join the Org's RSI Org
4. Ticket Assignee will then need to verify the user's RSI Dossier (checking their orgs & affiliations)
5. Ticket Assignee's will ensure that this user is not currently flagged by the network
6. Ticket Assignee's will then process that User's RSI Membership, and ensure they have their Primary Org set to the Org they are attempting to join.
7. Ticket Assignee will optionally set the initial Role if they need to change what the initial role should be for that member.
8. If the Org has the `New User Approval Requirement` turned on in their settings, the Onboarding ticket cannot be Approved until the required number of approvals have been collected on the ticket.
9. After everything has been completed, the Ticket Assignee can approve the onboarding ticket, and the system will then ensure that user recieves their Roles. 

* If other Role Assignment is required outside the Standard Rank Roles, these can be applied after the Onboarding Process utilizing the Discord or Member Management Tools in the Network Hub.

#### 4.1.2. Network Onboarding
After a user has been approved, a Network Onboarding ticket will be created for the user. This will sync all of their network roles and acceess to the user.
The new user will be evaluated by the system regarding their precense in any other discords to calculate Network Guest or Network Partner status. If the user is a Certified Member in at least one(1) Network Member Org, they then qualify for Network Member.
A ticket will be created for Onboarding them to the Network, which they must complete to gain access to any Network Tools.
1. The user will be evaluated by the system
2. If the user is a Certified Member of more than one(1) Network Member Org, they will be prompted to select their Primary Org.
3. Upon selection of a Primary Org, any Member Roles, Officer Roles, or Leader Roles will be stripped from that user in other Network Member Orgs.
4. The user will be prompted to Join the Network Hub RSI as a secondary Org.
5. After requirements are met, a Ticket Assignee (a representive from the Network Hub) would process their RSI Application to the Network Hub
6. The Ticket Assignee will then close the ticket.
7. When the ticket closes, the User will be assigned their Network Roles for all of the Network Member Org discords they are in.

* When a New Member Org completes their Onboarding, any existing users they have will skip the Primary Org Onboarding(Section 4.1.1) and will have to go through this Network Onboarding Process.
* Network Onboarding is not required of a User. However, Member Orgs should encorage their users to complete this process as this will allow Users to properly utilize all Network Features.
* If a user is in conflict and does not engage with the ticket to correct this, the relevant Leaderships will be contacted to correct the conflict.
* If a user's membership on the Network Hub's RSI has not been verified, the ticket will become Archived after two weeks(14 days). If it becomes archived, they will need to reach out to their Org's Leadership or the Network Hub's Leadership to finalize the ticket.

### 4.2. New Member Org Approval
If an Org wishes to become a registered Org with the Network, a User that has completed their Primary Org Onboarding can submit an application to Register their Org.
1. The Representive of the Org will need to provide the following details:
    * Name of Org
    * Org Tag
    * RSI Link
    * Discord Link
    * What status they are seeking with the network
2. A Vote will then be created where the Network Leadership Council will then convene on. A vote of at least 70% approval is required in order to be approved.
3. Upon approval, if they are Approved as a Network Member Org, they will then have an Onboarding Ticket created.

### 4.3. New Member Org Onboarding
* The only people who can be assigned to a Member Org Onboarding Ticket is a Developer.
1. The new Org will need to first invite the Network Terminal Bot to their Discord and the system will validate it.
2. After the Developer approves this has been completed, the Representive will then provide remaining information.
